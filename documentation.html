Electric Vehicle Visualization Documentation

Dataset: Washington State EV Population Data

---

Design Process

The visualization was developed through an iterative design process focused on improving clarity and usability. Early versions included multiple charts, including a scatterplot, which made the interface visually complex and difficult to interpret. Users were required to mentally connect different charts, which increased cognitive effort and reduced clarity.

To address this, the design was simplified into two coordinated views: a timeline on the left and a ranking chart on the right. This structure creates a clear relationship between user actions and results. The timeline acts as the input, while the ranking chart reflects the output. This change made the visualization more intuitive and easier to use.

Initial sketches were used to explore layout and interaction ideas before implementing the final version.

---

Rationale of Design Choices

Color was used intentionally to encode meaning. Purple represents all electric vehicles, teal represents battery electric vehicles, and blue represents plug-in hybrid vehicles. These choices are based on perception. Teal is commonly associated with sustainability, while blue reflects hybrid or mixed systems. Purple acts as a neutral aggregate color.

Inactive elements are shown in grey to reduce visual noise and direct attention to selected data. This creates a clear visual hierarchy and improves readability.

The interaction design focuses on direct manipulation. Users drag across the timeline to filter data, and the bar chart updates accordingly. Clicking a brand highlights it and updates the timeline to show that brand’s trend over time. This creates a clear connection between the two charts.

Logos were added to improve usability. Instead of relying only on text labels, users can recognize brands instantly through familiar visual symbols. This follows the principle that recognition is faster than recall, especially when scanning multiple items.

Gradients were used in the bar chart to reinforce ranking. Darker colors represent higher values, while lighter colors represent lower values. This allows users to quickly understand differences without relying only on numerical values.

---

Interaction Design

The visualization supports several interactive features:

  • Dragging across the timeline filters data by year range
  • Clicking a brand highlights it and updates the timeline
  • Hovering shows detailed values

The right chart is empty by default to encourage user interaction and make the relationship between charts clear.

---

Annotations and Context

Annotations were added to provide context for major events:

  • 2010: Nissan Leaf and Chevy Volt launch
  • 2017: Tesla Model 3 release
  • 2022: Inflation Reduction Act

These help explain changes in adoption trends and connect the data to real-world events.

---

Insights and Findings

The visualization was used to explore patterns in electric vehicle adoption and brand dominance over time.

  • EV adoption increases significantly after 2017
  • Tesla becomes the dominant brand in recent years
  • Earlier years show more balanced competition
  • Policy and product releases align with growth spikes

The timeline interaction allows comparison between different periods, revealing how brand rankings change over time.

---

Implementation Notes

The visualization was built using D3.js. The original dataset was large, so it was processed into a smaller aggregated JSON format. This reduced loading time significantly and ensured smooth performance while preserving key trends.
